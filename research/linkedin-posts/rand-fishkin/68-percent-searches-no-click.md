# 68% of searches end without a click

- **Author:** Rand Fishkin
- **URL:** https://sparktoro.com/blog/
- **Why this post:** New primary research (with Similarweb clickstream data), not a rehash of his 2019 study — shows he's still actively producing original data in 2026, not coasting on past reputation.

---

Using Similarweb's clickstream panel, Fishkin found that in the first four months of 2026, 68.01% of US Google searches ended without a click anywhere — not just without a click to a specific site, but without a click at all. He frames this as evidence Google is becoming a "walled garden," aided by AI features, instant answers, and UI elements that keep users inside the results page.
