# Three things decide who gets cited (not just who owns the data)

- **Author:** Kevin Indig
- **Why this post:** Part 2 of a series specifically framing "first-party data" as a defensible AI-citation asset — directly relevant to AI-powered SEO content production, since it's about what makes content worth producing in the first place, not just how to produce it faster. Companion piece to the "aggregator" post above — shows Indig building a connected body of research rather than one-off takes.

---

Publishing data only your product can produce is the most defensible AI citation asset you have. No competitor can copy a number your business generates as a byproduct.

The catch: owning the number may not win you the citation.

Publishing original data is necessary but not sufficient. Three things decide who gets cited:
1. The data itself, a figure only your product can produce.
2. How you structure it, so AI can lift the number in one read.
3. Whether AI already trusts your site for the topic.

In part 2 of a series about authority and its impact on AI Visibility, I look at using primary research as a lever. The bar to stand out is lower than you might think!
