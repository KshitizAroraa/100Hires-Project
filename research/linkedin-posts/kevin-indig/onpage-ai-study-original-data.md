# The bar for primary research is lower than you think

- **Author:** Kevin Indig
- **Why this post:** Cites a specific third-party study (OnPage AI scoring 150 top-3 Google pages across 50 keywords/10 verticals) with a precise, actionable threshold — publish 4+ original data points to beat most competitors. This is the kind of concrete, checkable claim the task brief asks for, not vague "add more data" advice.

---

The bar to beat when it comes to primary research is lower than you might think.

OnPage AI scored 150 top-3 Google pages across 50 keywords and 10 verticals on how much each page adds beyond its ranking cohort. Original data predicted the score better than any other trait, including length.

The number I keep flagging: top organic results carry only 4 unique data points on average.
- Pages with one or fewer figures scored 40.2 on information gain.
- Pages with 15 or more scored 62.1.

Publish more than 4 real, original figures, and you've cleared most of the field.
