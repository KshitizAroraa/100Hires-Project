# Aggregators can steal the citation your research earned

- **Author:** Kevin Indig
- **Why this post:** Directly challenges the common "just publish original research" advice with a specific, counterintuitive finding — that DATE and NUMBER entity types predict ChatGPT citations more than anything else, and that presentation/structure can beat who owns the data. Backed by a real chart (page-position decile citation analysis across 6 verticals, sourced from Growth Memo / Gauge).

---

Everyone says publish original research. But who tests whether AI cites the brand that produced the number or the page that surfaces it most cleanly?

The answer is uncomfortable: an aggregator can repackage your benchmark into an answer-ready page and collect the citation your research earned.

Owning the data gets you into the fight. It doesn't win it.

I found DATE and NUMBER are the entity types that predict ChatGPT citations most. A precise figure surfaced high on the page beats the same figure buried in narrative, even when the buried one came first. The aggregator wins on presentation and on the trust AI already assigns its domain.

The work doesn't stop at publishing. Structure the number for extraction, and build the off-site authority that puts your domain in AI's trusted set for the topic.
