# The grounding budget study

- **Author:** Dan Petrovic (DEJAN Marketing)
- **URL:** https://dejan.ai/blog/ai-spam/
- **Published:** 2025-12
- **Why this post:** The single most rigorous, most-cited piece of research on this entire list — analyzed 7,060 queries, 2,275 tokenized pages, and 883,262 grounding snippets, independently cited by Sitebulb, a 34-study meta-review, and other unrelated sources.

---

Petrovic's DEJAN team reverse-engineered how Google/Gemini's "grounding" system allocates content into AI answers. Key findings:
- Gemini allocates roughly 2,000 words of "grounding budget" per query, split across sources by rank
- The #1 organic result gets roughly 28% of that budget vs. ~13% for the #5 result
- Pages over 3,000 words see steep diminishing returns, with only ~13% of their content actually used
- The average extracted "grounding chunk" is about 15.5 words
- Its cosine similarity to the source page's full meaning averages 0.916 — a tiny extracted chunk still captures most of the source's meaning
