So, if you're doing SEO for a brand new website, one of the best things that you could do is actually create less content. I see this all the time. People start a brand new website and they just start publishing tons and tons of content. But the problem is that if you don't have existing website authority, you don't have backlinks. That content is a likely not going to perform and b it's not going to have stable rankings. You're not going to maintain those rankings. So there is sometimes what I would call newbie gains with a new website where you might be able to see some initial gains very quickly, but it won't last because unless you have the back links to fortify the website, those results are not going to stick. So, the best thing you can do is create three to five really good assets and then spend almost all of your time and all of your resources driving the authority of the site, driving links to the site, building that site authority as much as humanly possible because that's the thing that's going to help you not only drive results in the short term, but also help you maintain those results.
